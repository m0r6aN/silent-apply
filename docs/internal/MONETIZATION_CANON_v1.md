# 🔒 MONETIZATION CANON v1 (SilentApply)

## Purpose

Monetization exists to **support candidate control**, not to extract urgency.

**Monetization is not**

* a growth loop
* a conversion funnel
* a pressure mechanism
* a value judgment

**Prime mental model**

> “This is available if you want it.”

---

## 1) Core Principle (NON-NEGOTIABLE)

SilentApply must be:

* **fully usable**
* **fully respectable**
* **fully calm**

**without payment.**

Payment never unlocks *dignity*.

---

## 2) Free Forever (LOCKED)

These features are **never paid**:

* Quiet Link (public profile)
* Basic profile fields
* Proof links
* Recruiter Q&A (bounded)
* Booking (with reasonable limits)
* Resume upload (canonical resume)
* Resume download (if enabled by candidate)
* Profile visibility controls
* Basic analytics (view count, downloads count)

If any of these become paid, the product is broken.

---

## 3) Paid Surfaces (Allowed, Sealed)

Paid features must meet **all** of these criteria:

* additive
* optional
* non-urgent
* non-disruptive
* invisible to recruiters

### Canon-approved paid categories

#### 3.1 Resume Variants (Primary)

* AI-generated contextual resume variants
* Explicitly candidate-triggered
* Clearly labeled as contextual

**Framing**

> “Create a contextual version for this role.”

Not:

* “Increase your chances”
* “Beat the ATS”
* “Optimize to win”

---

#### 3.2 Advanced Availability Controls

Examples:

* custom buffers
* meeting length variations
* advanced scheduling rules

Framed as **control**, not advantage.

---

#### 3.3 Quiet Insights (Candidate-only)

Examples:

* aggregate recruiter interest trends
* question themes over time
* non-identifying signals

No scoring. No recommendations. No prescriptions.

---

## 4) Where Monetization May Appear (STRICT)

Allowed locations:

* Candidate **settings**
* Candidate **resume management**
* Candidate **account page**

**Not allowed**

* Quiet Link page
* Recruiter-facing surfaces
* Auth flow
* Q&A flow
* Booking flow

Recruiters must never know whether a candidate is paid.

---

## 5) How Monetization Is Presented (SEALED)

### Tone rules

* Factual
* Optional
* Calm
* No benefit inflation

### Allowed language

* “Available”
* “Optional”
* “If useful”

### Forbidden language

* “Upgrade”
* “Unlock”
* “Don’t miss”
* “Boost”
* “Stand out”

---

## 6) Pricing Structure (Canonical Shape)

* Simple
* Flat
* Monthly or per-use
* No annual pressure
* No countdowns
* No discounts

No “plans.”
No tier ladders.

Payment is a utility, not an identity.

---

## 7) Trial Behavior (If Any)

If trials exist:

* time-based
* no auto-conversion surprises
* clear end

Never:

* feature-lock mid-flow
* interrupt candidate actions

---

## 8) Failure Modes (Important)

If payment fails:

* core functionality remains
* paid features quietly disable
* no warnings on public surfaces

No embarrassment. No friction.

---

## 9) Analytics & Incentives (Prohibited)

Do **not**:

* A/B test monetization copy
* optimize for conversion
* introduce scarcity
* rank candidates

SilentApply is not a marketplace.

---

## 10) Canon Summary

Monetization in SilentApply should feel like:

* adding a tool to a workbench
* not being upsold at checkout

If a candidate ever thinks:

> “They’re pushing me.”

The system has violated canon.

---

## 🧠 Final State (Zoomed Out)

SilentApply now has:

* Quiet auth
* Quiet links
* Calm layout
* Bounded Q&A
* Respectful booking
* Ethical AI usage
* Invisible monetization

This is a **rare product posture**. It’s also defensible, coherent, and extremely hard to copy.
